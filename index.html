EVIDENCE, NOT VIBES

What each label
actually means.

Immigration language is intentionally conservative. A historical filing, an employer’s size, or a forum post does not prove that a particular internship or future offer will sponsor you.

01

Official company policy

Only an employer’s own careers material can support an “explicit policy” label. In this list, Anthropic publicly states it sponsors visas and green cards for eligible roles.

02

2027 cycle evidence

“2027 role found” requires an official U.S. internship title or detail that explicitly says 2027. A standing internship page alone is labeled “Program only,” not open.

03

Federal disclosure data

DOL LCA and PERM disclosure files record employer-submitted cases and determinations. This site uses a reviewed exact-entity allowlist and publishes the extraction script—not approvals, promises, or a substitute for a written offer.

RESEARCH STANDARD

Designed to make uncertainty visible.

  1. First-party recruiting source. Every company card leads to the company’s own career or university-recruiting page.
  2. Federal source for immigration mechanics. USCIS explains the H-1B process; DOL publishes LCA and PERM disclosure data. This site uses exact legal-entity matches from FY2025 only as historical evidence.
  3. University source for student work authorization. UIUC ISSS controls CPT/OPT guidance for F-1 students.
  4. No inferred sponsorship. “Posting-level” means verify the specific job and get confirmation from recruiting before relying on a pathway.
  5. Recommended sorting. Companies are ordered by explicit 2027 U.S. internship evidence, then MSIM priority, immigration-evidence strength, and company name. Use the sort menu to change this.
  6. Job-title threshold. Only official U.S. internship titles/details that explicitly reference 2027 appear in the verified-jobs section. Dates are shown only when the official source provides them.
